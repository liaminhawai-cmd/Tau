The physical game

An abstract fighting game based on sumo, judo and wrestling.

You’re trying to push, block or bait the other piece off the edge of the board. I wanted to make the fighting game equivalent of chess, so it’s simple deterministic rules, continuous movement, no straight lines. I built the digital version first, but it started as a metal board and magnetic tripods on my table, and that’s the set I’m making now.

Free to play in your browser right now. Easier to understand by playing than reading.

How it plays

The rules are simple. Two pieces, one each, and the only way to win is to push your opponent off the edge. Each turn you keep one foot planted and swing the other two around it in an arc. The movement’s continuous so you can stop wherever you like, but you can only cross one line on the board per move. That limit is what makes it a fighting game and opens up lots of fun counterintuitive sweeps and throws.

  • Two pieces, a board of concentric rings, essentially two rules.
  • Magnets in the feet and a metal board, so the pieces move smoothly without momentum turning it into a dexterity game.
  • Same rules as the free digital version, so you can learn it in your browser.

# Giant Tau piece — 1 m board, printed on an Ender 3

The real piece (`tripod smoove 2021.stl`, the Ø266.7 mm-board tripod) scaled by **exactly 3.75×**
(1 game unit: 2 mm → 7.5 mm, so a Ø1000 mm board), then split into a hub and three identical legs
that click together. Generated by `../tau_giant.py` — regenerate any time with
`python3 tau_giant.py`; every part is CSG-built and verified watertight.

**No magnets.** Per the big-model plan (cost model v5): each foot takes a **domed button-head
bolt seated head-down** — mass low at the three outer points so the piece is bottom-heavy, and
the polished steel dome is the sliding contact. 3 bolts per piece, 6 per set.

Assembled piece: **322 × 282 × 202 mm**, foot circle radius 173.2 mm — matches the digital
`footR` (23.095 u × 7.5 mm) on a 1 m board.

## Foot bolts

The scaled foot tip is only Ø8.4, so each foot carries a printed **boss** (frustum Ø26→Ø14 ×
30 mm) with:
- a recessed **seat** the head's shoulder pulls up against — the dome cap protrudes **4.0 mm**
  below the plastic, so the piece rides on three steel domes and the print never touches the board;
- a snug **pilot bore** (Ø8.9 for M10 coarse) the bolt **self-taps** into. Drive it with the hex
  key until the shoulder seats. The piece's weight rests on the seat; the thread only keeps the
  bolt from dropping out when the piece is lifted.

Default hardware: **M10 × 40 button head** (≈30 g each → ~90 g of steel per piece, low and at the
rim — exactly where anti-tip mass wants to be). `BOLT='M8'` in `tau_giant.py` regenerates the
lighter variant (Ø7.2 pilot, M8 × 30). Galvanised/zinc domes are smooth enough; stainless is
smoother. If a bolt ever strips its bore, a drop of epoxy in the pilot re-sets it.

## The joint

Each leg's cut face is a concave spherical cup that nests over a matching convex stub on the hub
(both faces come from the same cutting sphere, 0.15 mm apart — self-aligning). A keyed
rectangular tenon (12.1 × 13.4 × 22 mm) enters a mortise with **0.20 mm clearance per side**, and
a 0.45 mm-proud retention dome on the tenon top pops past a 0.25 mm-deep dimple in the mortise
roof — that's the click. Push straight in until it snaps. A drop of glue in the mortise makes it
permanent if you want.

**Print the coupon pair first.** If the click is too tight/loose on your machine, edit
`LOCK_CLEAR` (and `BUMP_PROUD`) at the top of `tau_giant.py`, regenerate, re-coupon. This is the
same dial-in flow as the rim segments.

## Print settings (starting point)

- **Leg — lay it flat on its side** (it's a planar arc only 22 mm thick). Low, stable, fast, and
  the layer direction is strongest along the leg. Tree/organic supports on, touching buildplate
  only mostly suffices; brim optional.
  Do NOT print it standing on the foot — tall, tiny bed contact, layer lines across the thin
  ankle (the weak direction).
- **Hub — dome up, flat as it comes.** The three stubs angle down/outward: enable supports
  (tree supports handle them with little scarring on the cut faces; the mating faces tolerate
  a bit of roughness because the tenon carries the fit).
- 0.4 nozzle, 0.2 layers, **4 walls** (legs are slender — walls matter more than infill),
  15 % gyroid infill, PLA or PETG.
- Rough material per piece: ~3 × 45 g (legs) + ~65 g (hub) ≈ **200 g** at these settings.

## Board reference

1 m board = drop-off radius 500 mm. Rings at 300 mm and 399.8 mm radius (game `rings` 40 u and
53.3 u × 7.5). Start-dot hub offset 229.5 mm (`startX` 30.597 u × 7.5). Foot pad contact stays
rule-honest at the scaled size.
